What I Do

UX Research

Anthropology

My approach to user research draws on Four-Field Anthropology. This is an interdisciplinary approach involving the biological, socio-cultural, archaeological, and linguistic sciences.

Qualitative Research

I specialize in employing holistic and empirical qualitative methods like ethnography, situated action, artifact analysis, participant observation, needs assessment and heuristic evaluation.

Human-Computer Interaction

We are all cyborgs now. And at the heart of every human-computer interaction is a human communicating with another human. How do we use the things we use to do the things we do?

Compact Localized Augmented Work-Station (CLAWS)

I am working as a user experience specialist with a large team of students of two design challenges for NASA, SUITS and X-Hab. We are designing augmented reality interfaces for in-helmet information processing on the ISS, the Moon, and eventually Mars. In addition, I am coordinating Human-in-the-Loop usability testing of our prototype in our own student operated usability lab.

Ours is 1 of 10 teams invited to demonstrate our prototype at the Johnson Space Center in Houston this April. Our modules are solving problems related to navigation, geological sampling and field notes, EVAs (extravehicular activities), telemetry coordination and mission control.
